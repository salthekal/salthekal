## The short version

I'm a physicist who ended up running data and operations for a multi-city last-mile logistics 3PL in Saudi Arabia, where I design and ship the software that runs the business. My background is experimental particle physics — thesis work on the **LUXE experiment at DESY** while at Padova, rigorous training in measurement and uncertainty that I now apply to a very different kind of problem. These days I split my time between shipping production software, the **Machine Learning Specialization from DeepLearning.AI**, and quietly exploring what AIOps looks like when it's more than a buzzword.

### ⚛️ &nbsp; [luxe-digit](https://github.com/salthekal/luxe-digit)

ML-augmented detector digitization pipeline for the **LUXE Gamma Beam Profiler** at DESY. Simulates the full analog chain — charge collection, noise, crosstalk, amplification, ADC — then adds a PyTorch feature extractor with MC-dropout uncertainty on top of the original Geant4 + ROOT pipeline. Built on prior work by Pietro Grutta (INFN Padova) as part of my MSc thesis.

## What I'm actually thinking about

I've been thinking a lot about what it takes for machine learning to move from research notebooks into operational systems where it actually gets used. My current role is a daily education in the uncooperative side of that problem — messy multi-platform data, regulations that change mid-quarter, and the fact that the best model in the world is worthless if the people who have to rely on it can't trust it.

My LUXE work taught me a healthy respect for careful error propagation and uncertainty quantification — the kind of thing the operations world tends to ignore right up until it matters. Bridging those two sensibilities is the thread running through most of what I build these days. If that's the kind of thing you care about too, I'd enjoy hearing from you.
